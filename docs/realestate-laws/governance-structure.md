# Governance Structure — Loi 18-00

## Overview

Every copropriété has three mandatory governance bodies:

```
Assemblée Générale des copropriétaires
        │ elects (3/4 vote)
        ▼
    Syndic + Adjoint
        │ manages
        ▼
  Common areas & syndicat operations

[Complex only]
Conseil Syndical (composed of all individual syndics)
        │ elects from among itself (absolute majority)
        ▼
    Syndic du Conseil Syndical + Adjoint
        │ manages
        ▼
  Global common areas of the complex
```

---

## 1. Syndicat des copropriétaires (Article 13)

The **syndicat** is the legal entity that groups *all co-owners* of a single building/residence:
- Acquires legal personality (*personnalité morale*) and financial autonomy automatically from the date of the first title transfer in the building
- Purpose: conservation, maintenance, and administration of common areas; provision of collective services
- Can sue and be sued, including against individual co-owners
- Is managed by the Assemblée Générale and represented by the Syndic

One syndicat per building/residence. In a complex with multiple buildings, each building has **its own syndicat**.

---

## 2. Assemblée Générale (Articles 15–16 undecies)

The supreme decision-making body. All co-owners are members.

### Frequency
- **Ordinary AG**: at least once per year, within **30 days** of the end of the fiscal year (Article 16 ter)
- **Extraordinary AG**: whenever necessary

### Convocation
- Notice sent by **registered letter with acknowledgement**, bailiff, or any legal notification method
- Minimum **15 days** before the meeting (Article 16 quinquies)
- Must include: date, time, place, full agenda
- Documents (accounts, budget, contracts) made available **15 days** before (Article 16 septies), contracts 3 days before

### Quorum
- First session: valid if at least **50% of co-owners** are present or represented (Article 18)
- If quorum not reached: second session held within **30 days**, valid regardless of attendance, decisions by simple majority

### Proxy voting
- A proxy may represent at most **3 co-owners** whose combined shares do not exceed **10%** of total votes (Article 16 decies)
- Proxy must be in writing

### Co-owner in default
- A co-owner who has not paid their charges is **excluded from attending** the AG (Article 16 quinquies)

### After the AG
- Syndic must notify all co-owners of the minutes within **8 days** of decisions (Article 16 nonies, 30)
- Decisions challenged by any injured party must be appealed within **2 months** of notification (Article 59 duodecies)

## 3. Syndic and Adjoint (Articles 19, 26–27 ter)

### Election
- Elected by the AG at **3/4 majority** of all co-owners' votes (Article 19)
- Term: **2 years**, renewable
- Can be a co-owner (syndic bénévole) or a professional firm (syndic professionnel)
- Syndic and adjoint cannot preside over the AG (Article 17)

### Mandatory duties (Article 26)
- Execute AG decisions
- Collect co-owner contributions (charges)
- Issue discharge receipts to co-owners
- Maintain accounts and report financial situation to co-owners at least every **6 months**
- Open and maintain a **separate bank account** in the syndicat's name — all income and expenses go through it
- Maintain the syndicat's archives and hand them over to the successor
- Represent the syndicat in legal proceedings

### End of mandate (Article 26 bis)
- Resignation (must notify owners and convene AG within 30 days — Article 26 ter)
- Mandate expiry (unless renewed)
- Revocation by AG (same 3/4 majority — Article 27 bis)
- Dissolution (if syndic is a legal entity)
- Death

### Liability (Article 27)
Syndic and adjoint are personally liable for failing to fulfill their duties.

---

## 4. Conseil Syndical — complex only (Article 29)

Only exists for **ensembles immobiliers** managed by multiple syndicats.

### Composition
- Formed automatically (*de plein droit*) — no vote needed
- Members: **all syndics and their adjoints** of all syndicats in the complex (ex officio)

### Internal leadership
- At its first meeting, the council elects among its members (by **absolute majority**) a **Syndic du Conseil Syndical** and an adjoint — 2-year mandate, renewable

### Meetings
- Convened at the request of **2 or more members**, at least **once every 6 months**, and whenever needed
- Convocation and meeting rules same as a regular AG

### Decisions (Article 29 bis)
- Taken by **absolute majority** of syndics and adjoints present or represented

### Scope
- Manages the **global common areas** of the complex (parking, gardens, shared infrastructure)
- Expenses for global common areas are charged to **all syndicats** of the complex proportionally (Article 29 quater)
- Members receive **no remuneration** (Article 29 ter)

### Resistance to crisis
- Even when a provisional administrator is appointed to one syndicat, the conseil syndical continues functioning (Article 59 quater)

---

## 5. Provisional administrator (Articles 59 bis–59 decies)

When a syndicat cannot pay its debts or maintain the property:
- President of the court of first instance may appoint a **provisional administrator** on request of the syndic or **10% of co-owners** (Article 59 bis)
- Mandate: max **1 year**, with same powers as the syndic
- Suspends ongoing legal actions by creditors for the mandate duration (Article 59 septies)
- Must convene the AG before mandate ends to approve budget and elect new syndic (Article 59 nonies)
- Syndic, their spouse, relatives, or employees cannot serve as provisional administrator
