# Charges and Finances — Loi 18-00

## Types of charges

### 1. Charges communes générales (Article 36)
All co-owners contribute to the conservation, maintenance, and administration of the **general common areas** proportionally to their *quote-part indivise*.

### 2. Charges des services collectifs (Article 36)
Each co-owner contributes to collective services (elevator, cleaning, security, etc.) proportionally to the **utility** those services provide to their private lot — not necessarily their quote-part.

### 3. Charges spéciales (Article 16 undecies)
When a part of the building or equipment is reserved for exclusive use by a **subset of co-owners**, only those co-owners bear those charges. The *règlement de copropriété* can limit the vote on those charges to the concerned owners only.

### 4. Complex global charges (Article 29 quater)
In a *ensemble immobilier*, the cost of managing and maintaining the **global common areas** is borne by all sub-syndicats proportionally.

---

## Budget cycle (Articles 21, 24)

1. AG approves the **budget prévisionnel** (provisional budget) by 3/4 majority
2. Budget fixes: total charges for the year, charge ceiling, provision for major works reserve
3. AG meets within **6 months** of the previous fiscal year-end to set the reserve provision
4. Each co-owner pays periodic *provisions* (installments) as set by the AG
5. If urgent additional spending is needed mid-year, the syndic can request supplementary contributions — subject to ratification at the next AG

---

## Syndic's financial obligations (Article 26)

The syndic must:
- Open a **dedicated bank account** in the syndicat's name — not a sub-account of the syndic's personal/company accounts
- Deposit all received funds immediately
- All syndicat income and expenses must flow through this account
- Report the financial position to co-owners at least every **6 months**
- Present full accounts to the AG

---

## Collecting charges (Articles 25, 25 bis)

- The syndic collects charges **without needing prior AG authorization** for each collection (Article 25)
- If a co-owner misses a payment: syndic sends formal notice (*mise en demeure*)
- If no response: syndic files an *injonction de payer* (payment order) with the *tribunal de première instance* (Article 25 bis)

### Fast-track payment order (Article 25 bis)
The president of the court issues the order based on:
1. AG minutes approving the charge amount for that year
2. Statement of the debtor's arrears, approved by the syndic
3. Certificate of ownership proving the debtor's share of common areas
4. Proof that a formal notice was sent

The order carries **provisional enforceability** (executes immediately pending appeal).

### Court timeline (Article 36 bis)
The court must rule on the injonction de payer within **3 months**.

---

## Legal security: forced mortgage (Articles 40, 40 bis, 40 ter)

The syndicat holds a **statutory mortgage** (*hypothèque forcée*) over each co-owner's private lot as security for charge debts (under Article 171 of the Code des droits réels, Loi 39-08).

Process when a co-owner refuses to register the mortgage:
1. Syndic prepares a debt statement + certified copy of the AG decision setting the budget
2. Sends by registered letter (or bailiff) to the co-owner's declared address
3. After **8 days** without response, syndic requests the court president to order registration of the forced mortgage
4. Once the debt is paid, syndic can request release and cancellation of the mortgage without restriction

---

## Reserve fund (Articles 37 bis, 37 ter)

- A **compte de réserve** (reserve account) may be created by AG decision (3/4 majority) to cover unusual or urgent expenses
- All co-owners contribute periodically — amounts and conditions set by the AG
- In an emergency, the syndic may draw on the reserve but must notify each co-owner in writing
- Co-owners must pay their reserve contributions by the set date (unless otherwise agreed)

---

## Charge disputes (Article 35 ter)

If a co-owner believes their charge allocation exceeds their *quote-part*, they can petition the competent court to revise it. The case is brought against the syndicat des copropriétaires, with the syndic present.

Charge distribution changes can only be decided by the AG under Articles 21 and 24 — not unilaterally by the syndic.

---

## Sale of a private lot (Article 42)

When a co-owner sells their apartment:
- Outstanding charges owed by the seller become the responsibility of the new buyer from the date of transfer
- The syndicat can enforce payment from the new owner under Articles 25 and 25 bis

---

## Statute of limitations (Article 43)

Syndicat claims against co-owners expire **5 years** from the date the amounts were approved by the AG.

## Key figures at a glance

| Rule | Value |
|------|-------|
| AG financial report frequency | Every 6 months minimum |
| Annual AG deadline | Within 30 days of fiscal year-end |
| Court payment order deadline | Within 3 months (Art. 36 bis) |
| Forced mortgage response window | 8 days after notice |
| Statute of limitations on charges | 5 years from AG approval |
| AG challenge deadline | 2 months from notification |
